# Boundary Escalation Reference

Use when the problem is not misunderstanding but repeated disrespect, ignored limits, coercion, or unsafe interaction.

## Escalation ladder

| Level | Signal | Goal | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | one-off tension | repair | name impact, return to topic |
| 1 | repeated sarcasm / interruption | reset mode | state preferred mode and ask to restart |
| 2 | repeated old grievances / blame loops | narrow scope | one topic only, no historical pile-up |
| 3 | insults, contempt, mockery, late-night message flooding | protect boundary | state limit, pause condition, and re-entry time |
| 4 | boundary ignored repeatedly, repeated digital flooding, public blame, intimidation | reduce exposure | pause, written clarity, mute/block if needed, third party, formal channel |
| 5 | threats, violence, stalking, coercive control | safety | exit, trusted support, emergency/professional resources |

## Pause guidance

For ordinary emotional escalation, a pause can be useful. Suggested range: 20-60 minutes. The pause should include a re-entry plan:

> 我们先停 30 分钟。30 分钟后只讨论一个问题：X。目标不是翻旧账，而是决定下一步怎么做。

For important unresolved issues, schedule a revisit within 24-48 hours. Avoid indefinite silence unless safety requires distance.

## Digital flooding and shared-space boundaries

Repeated late-night messages, dozens of rapid texts, repeated calls, public tagging, or switching channels after a pause request are boundary signals, not just "communication style."

Use a channel boundary when the exchange itself becomes the problem:

- state when you will respond, not only that you are upset;
- stop replying after the stated pause condition;
- mute or block temporarily if messages continue after the boundary;
- keep records when there are threats, public accusations, work disputes, or repeated boundary violations;
- if you share a home, avoid escalating in person late at night; choose a calmer time, public/common area, or third party when needed;
- if the other person threatens harm, blocks exit, follows, stalks, or uses coercive control, skip ordinary re-entry planning and prioritize safety.

## Re-entry conditions

A useful re-entry condition is concrete:

- discuss one topic;
- no insults, mockery, threats, or翻旧账;
- each side gets uninterrupted time;
- end with a next step, not a verdict on character.

## Boundary templates

### First reset

> 我们现在有点从事情本身滑到互相攻击了。我愿意继续聊，但希望只讨论当前这件事。

### Clear limit

> 我可以接受不同意见，但不能接受讽刺和人身攻击。如果继续这样，我会先暂停这次对话。

### Pause with re-entry

> 我现在继续聊会变成防御。我们先停 30 分钟，之后只讨论 X 和下一步。

### Digital flooding

> 我看到你连续发了很多条。我今晚不会继续逐条回复。明晚 8 点我们只谈 X 和下一步。如果继续半夜刷屏或切换渠道追问，我会先静音，等约定时间再回到这个问题。

### Repeated violation

> 我已经说过我不接受这种表达方式。如果继续出现，我会结束这次沟通。之后我们可以在更合适的场合，或者找第三方一起谈。

### Work formalization

> 这个问题我们已经多次口头讨论但没有收敛。我会把当前分歧、风险和建议下一步写成文档，方便大家确认责任和决策。

## Safety caveat

If there is credible threat, violence, stalking, coercive control, or self-harm risk, do not optimize phrasing to keep the conversation going. Prioritize safety, trusted support, and appropriate emergency/professional resources.
