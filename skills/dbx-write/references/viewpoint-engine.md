# Viewpoint Engine

A viewpoint article is not a decorated answer. It is a controlled pressure system: one judgment meets resistance, digs into its own premise, and leaves the reader with a sharper model.

## Thesis before topic

A topic says what the piece is about.

```text
AI 对写作的影响。
```

A thesis says what the piece will prove, complicate, or make felt.

```text
AI 真正改变写作的地方，不是帮人省掉打字，而是让没有判断的人更快暴露出没有判断。
```

A strong thesis usually contains:

- a visible object: AI writing, frontend architecture, career choice, attention, money, happiness;
- a judgment: what is actually changing, broken, misunderstood, overvalued, undervalued;
- a tension: why readers may not already agree;
- a boundary: when the judgment holds and when it does not.

## The seven cuts

Use these cuts to turn a seed idea into an argument.

### Cut 1: Say the claim with teeth

Remove hedge fog.

Weak:

```text
我觉得长期主义可能挺重要的。
```

Sharper:

```text
很多人谈长期主义，其实是在用一个漂亮词逃避短期反馈。
```

### Cut 2: Find the hidden premise

Ask:

- What must be true for this claim to hold?
- What does the claim assume about people, systems, incentives, time, risk, or attention?
- Which premise would an intelligent opponent attack?

### Cut 3: Find the mechanism

Do not stay at value judgment. Ask how it works.

- What changes first?
- Which feedback loop appears?
- Which cost moves from one person to another?
- Which constraint becomes visible?
- Which metric lies?

### Cut 4: Find the price

Insight without price becomes slogan.

Name one or more:

- time cost;
- social cost;
- opportunity cost;
- emotional cost;
- maintenance cost;
- loss of optionality;
- loss of comforting illusion.

### Cut 5: Find the counterexample

A real viewpoint survives contact with exception.

Ask:

- Where is this wrong?
- Who should ignore this advice?
- What would change my mind?
- What does the opposite view understand correctly?

### Cut 6: Find the reader's pressure point

A reader resonates when the article names a pressure they already carry but have not articulated.

Ask:

- What has the reader seen at work, in life, or in themselves?
- What sentence would make them stop scrolling?
- What frustration, shame, hope, or confusion does this clarify?
- Which scene makes the abstract point felt?

### Cut 7: Return with a harder judgment

The ending should not restate the opening. It should return after being tested.

Opening:

```text
AI 让写作门槛降低了。
```

Earned ending:

```text
AI 降低的是出稿门槛，不是表达门槛。真正的门槛从“能不能写”移动到了“有没有东西值得写”。
```

## Insight ladder

Use this ladder to check depth.

| Level | Output | Problem |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Topic | "聊聊工程效率" | No judgment |
| Correct opinion | "工程效率很重要" | True but empty |
| Mechanism | "效率问题常常是反馈路径问题" | Starts to explain |
| Tradeoff | "缩短反馈也会放大错误方向" | Gains boundary |
| Human layer | "人讨厌慢反馈，是因为慢反馈让努力像掉进雾里" | Gains resonance |
| Operational heuristic | "先优化最频繁、最可验证、最少争议的反馈环" | Becomes usable |
| Earned sentence | "效率不是快，是错误更早变便宜" | Becomes memorable |

## False depth detector

Reject a "deep" paragraph when it only does one of these:

- replaces concrete nouns with larger abstractions;
- says both sides have value but makes no judgment;
- adds "本质上" without showing a mechanism;
- adds history,时代, or philosophy without changing the argument;
- turns a practical tension into personal growth mist;
- concludes with "保持开放" when the article never risked a stance.

## Reader resonance without flattery

Resonance comes from precision, not from telling the reader they are special.

Prefer:

```text
很多工程师不是不想重构，而是不想再一次证明“先还债”比“先交付”更像在拖进度。
```

Avoid:

```text
作为优秀的工程师，我们都应该重视长期价值。
```

## Working forms

### Thesis compression

```text
I am writing about: ...
Most people think: ...
I think the harder truth is: ...
This matters because: ...
The mechanism is: ...
The price is: ...
The boundary is: ...
The reader has felt this when: ...
```

### Opposition test

```text
A smart opponent would say: ...
They are right about: ...
They are wrong because: ...
My revised claim is: ...
```

### Scene bridge

```text
Concrete scene: ...
What the scene seems to show: ...
What it actually exposes: ...
Bigger mechanism: ...
Reader takeaway: ...
```
