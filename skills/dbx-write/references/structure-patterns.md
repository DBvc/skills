# Structure Patterns

Choose structure by the argument's dominant pressure, not by habit.

## 1. Blade

Use for one strong viewpoint that should be cut deeper layer by layer.

```markdown
# Title

Opening tension: a sentence or scene that exposes the common misunderstanding.

## 先把话说重一点

State the thesis clearly.

## 第一层：大家看见的是 X

Name the surface view.

## 第二层：真正起作用的是 Y

Explain mechanism.

## 第三层：代价在哪里

Name cost, boundary, or counterexample.

## 所以问题不是 A，而是 B

Return with a stronger and more precise thesis.

Ending sentence.
```

Best for:

- opinion essays;
- technical philosophy;
- career reflections;
- AI/software impact pieces.

Failure mode:

- every section repeats the same claim with different words.

## 2. Bridge

Use when connecting a technical idea to human, organizational, or product meaning.

```markdown
# Title

Concrete technical scene.

## 这个问题表面上是技术问题

Explain the technical object.

## 但它真正改变的是协作方式

Explain organizational or human mechanism.

## 为什么以前不明显

Historical or structural reason, only if useful.

## 新的判断边界

What the reader should decide differently.

## 留下的限制

What this does not solve.
```

Best for:

- frontend architecture;
- engineering efficiency;
- AI adoption;
- developer tools;
- software methodology.

## 3. Case

Use when the article grows from one example.

```markdown
# Title

Scene or case.

## 当时的问题

Describe concrete situation.

## 我原来的判断

Show old model.

## 真正的问题

Mechanism and hidden premise.

## 后来我会怎么做

Method, heuristic, or decision rule.

## 这件事的边界

Where the lesson stops.
```

Best for:

- engineering lessons;
- retrospectives;
- personal growth without sermon;
- decision-making reflections.

## 4. Argument

Use when the reader may disagree and evidence matters.

```markdown
# Title

Claim.

## 反对意见为什么合理

Steelman the other side.

## 我不同意的地方

Evidence or mechanism.

## 关键变量

What changes the decision.

## 我的判断

Recommendation or position.

## 什么时候我会改主意

Boundary and falsifiability.
```

Best for:

- technical choices;
- architecture tradeoffs;
- AI tool adoption;
- career decisions;
- public debate.

## 5. Reflection

Use for personal随笔 and self-understanding.

```markdown
# Title

A small scene, repeated habit, or sentence.

## 我以前怎么理解它

Old frame.

## 后来让我不舒服的地方

Contradiction.

## 真正改变的不是答案

New frame.

## 我现在怎么和它相处

Concrete change, not sermon.

Ending image or quiet sentence.
```

Best for:

- personal essays;
- happiness, acceptance, metacognition;
- work/life reflection;
- attention, ambition, anxiety.

## 6. Field note

Use for short posts and compact observations.

```markdown
# Title or first sentence

Observation.

The surprising interpretation.

One concrete example.

Boundary or implication.

Memorable final line.
```

Best for:

- social posts;
- short newsletter notes;
- thought fragments.

## Section progression tests

Each section should do at least one new job:

- introduce a real scene;
- sharpen the thesis;
- explain a mechanism;
- add evidence;
- name a tradeoff;
- answer a counterargument;
- shift the reader's emotional understanding;
- make the idea operational.

Delete or merge a section if its only job is:

- restating the title;
- listing generic benefits;
- transitioning with no content;
- summarizing what was already obvious.

## Opening moves

Good openings:

- a concrete contradiction;
- a short scene;
- a blunt thesis;
- a specific question that carries tension;
- a sentence that names a familiar but unnamed discomfort.

Weak openings:

- "随着时代的发展";
- dictionary definitions;
- broad historical background;
- "最近很多人在讨论";
- "在当今社会";
- motivational throat-clearing.

## Ending moves

Good endings:

- return to the opening with changed meaning;
- name the boundary;
- give a compact decision heuristic;
- leave a concrete image;
- state the refined thesis.

Weak endings:

- "综上所述";
- "让我们一起";
- "未来可期";
- generic optimism;
- moral elevation that the article did not earn.
